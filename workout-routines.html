🤸 Mary's Movement Guide

Two short routines — one for the office, one for home — with pictures for every step.

Mom, these are gentle and joint-friendly, and they double as bone-builders — which matters while you're on hormone-blocker medicine. Move at your pace, breathe, and never push into pain. Each picture shows the movement; the steps tell you how.

💼 The Office Routine (~5 min, every 90 min at your desk)

Wrist & finger stretch

1Wrist & Finger Stretch

  1. Stretch one arm out, palm down. With your other hand, gently bend the wrist down, then up.
  2. Hold each direction for 5 slow seconds.
  3. Spread your fingers wide, then make a soft fist. Repeat a few times.
Both handsRelief for all that typing — keeps your hands loose and comfortable.
Seated spinal twist

2Seated Spinal Twist

  1. Sit tall, feet flat on the floor.
  2. Place your right hand on the outside of your left thigh.
  3. Gently turn your upper body to the left, look over your shoulder. Hold 10 seconds.
  4. Slowly return to center, then twist to the other side.
2 each sideUnwinds the lower back from sitting and wakes up your spine.
Chin tuck

3Chin Tucks

  1. Sit tall, eyes forward.
  2. Gently draw your chin straight back (like making a "double chin"). You'll feel a light stretch at the back of the neck.
  3. Hold 5 seconds, release. Keep it slow and easy.
5 timesCounters "screen neck" and eases tension headaches.
Seated leg extension

4Seated Leg Extensions

  1. Sit tall, hold the sides of your chair.
  2. Straighten one leg out in front until it's level. Hold 3 seconds.
  3. Lower slowly and switch legs.
8 each legStrengthens the muscles around your knees — great for dancing and stairs.
Standing calf raise

5Standing Calf Raises

  1. Stand behind your chair, holding the back for balance.
  2. Slowly rise up onto the balls of your feet.
  3. Hold for 2 seconds at the top, then lower with control.
10–12A weight-bearing bone-builder for your lower legs, and it boosts circulation after sitting.

🏠 The At-Home Routine (~15–20 min, 3–4 days a week)

A gentle full-body set: a little strength, a little balance, and — your favorite — some dancing. Start with the wall push-up and sit-to-stand for strength, flow into balance, then turn on music and move.
Wall push-up

1Wall Push-Ups

  1. Stand arm's length from a wall, palms flat on it at shoulder height.
  2. Slowly bend your elbows and lean your chest toward the wall.
  3. Gently push back to the start. Keep your body in a straight line.
8–10Builds upper-body and arm strength gently — easy on the shoulders.
Sit-to-stand

2Sit-to-Stands (Chair Squats)

  1. Sit toward the front of a sturdy chair, feet flat and hip-width apart.
  2. Lean slightly forward and stand up — use your legs, hands only if needed.
  3. Slowly lower back down with control. That's one.
8–12The single best move for strong legs, hips, and bones — and for getting up easily for years to come.
Heel-to-toe walk

3Heel-to-Toe Balance Walk

  1. Stand near a counter or wall you can touch for safety.
  2. Walk a straight line, placing the heel of one foot just in front of the toes of the other.
  3. Take 10 slow, steady steps, eyes looking ahead.
10 steps × 2Sharpens balance and helps prevent falls — and it's wonderful for dancing footwork.
Standing hip march

4Standing Hip Marches

  1. Stand tall, holding a counter or chair with one hand.
  2. Slowly lift one knee up toward hip height.
  3. Lower it gently and lift the other knee — like a slow, controlled march.
10 each sideStrengthens hips and core and trains the balance you use every time you dance.
Dance / step-touch

5Dance It Out 💃

  1. Put on 2–3 songs you love.
  2. Start with a simple step-touch: step right, tap left foot in; step left, tap right in.
  3. Add your arms, sway, turn — let your body move however feels good.
2–3 songsThis is the one you crave — joyful cardio that lifts your mood, your heart, and your spirit all at once.
Cool-down stretch

6Gentle Cool-Down

  1. Stand tall and reach both arms overhead, taking a slow breath in.
  2. Breathe out as you fold forward slightly, letting your arms and head hang loose (bend knees softly).
  3. Roll back up slowly, one vertebra at a time. Repeat 3 times.
3 slow roundsReleases the muscles, calms your system, and discharges that last bit of energy for great sleep.

⚕️ Move smart, Mom: These are gentle by design, but please get your doctor's okay before starting a new routine. Stop if anything causes pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath. If you had surgery or radiation on one side, ease into the arm and shoulder movements and don't force the range — let it open up gradually. Always keep a sturdy chair, counter, or wall within reach for balance moves. Slow and steady wins this one. 💛
