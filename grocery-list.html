🛒 Mary's Grocery List

Real food to fuel a full work week, dancing, and caring for the people you love.

Mom, this list is built around your real life: long days at the desk, the dancing you love, being there for your friend, and staying strong in your remission. It's plant-forward — honoring the way you've been eating — with steady protein for muscle, extra love for your bones and joints (since hormone-blocker medicine can be hard on them), and the colorful, fiber-rich, anti-inflammatory foods that survivor nutrition leans on. Aim for a serving of protein at every meal, lots of color, and water through the day.

🌱 Protein — your plant-forward foundation

Plant proteins — eat these freely

Beans, lentils, soy, nuts and seeds give protein plus the fiber that survivor diets are built on. Whole soy foods like tofu and edamame are considered safe — and possibly protective — for hormone-receptor breast cancer survivors.

  • Lentils (dry or canned)
  • Canned beans (black, chickpea)
  • Extra-firm tofu
  • Tempeh
  • Edamame (fresh/frozen)
  • Unsweetened soy milk
  • Ground flaxseed (lignans)
  • Chia & hemp seeds
  • Walnuts & almonds
  • Pumpkin seeds
  • Natural peanut/almond butter
  • Quinoa (a complete protein)

If & when you eat animal foods — optional

Entirely your call. If you add some, these are gentle, high-quality choices. Fatty fish brings the omega-3s that calm AI-related joint stiffness.

  • Eggs
  • Plain Greek or soy yogurt
  • Salmon (fresh, frozen, canned)
  • Canned tuna / sardines
  • Skinless chicken
  • Cottage cheese

🥦 Vegetables — bones, joints & color

Leafy greens, cruciferous & bright vegetables

Greens give calcium and vitamin K for bones; cruciferous veggies (broccoli, kale, cabbage) are a cornerstone of survivor nutrition; bright veggies bring antioxidants that calm joint inflammation from dancing and standing.

  • Baby spinach
  • Kale or collard greens
  • Broccoli
  • Bell peppers
  • Carrots
  • Sweet potatoes
  • Tomatoes
  • Cucumber
  • Bagged salad mix
  • Frozen mixed veg (quick!)
  • Onions & garlic
  • Mushrooms

🍓 Fruit — recovery & sweetness

Anti-inflammatory & potassium-rich

Berries fight the soreness of an active day; bananas and oranges replace what you sweat out dancing.

  • Blueberries (fresh/frozen)
  • Strawberries
  • Bananas
  • Oranges / clementines
  • Apples
  • Avocados
  • Cherries (anti-inflammatory)
  • Lemons (for water)

🌾 Smart carbs — all-day fuel

Whole grains & slow energy

These release energy slowly, so you stay sharp through the afternoon instead of reaching for the candy dish.

  • Old-fashioned oats
  • Whole-grain bread
  • Brown rice
  • Quinoa
  • Whole-wheat pasta
  • Whole-grain crackers
  • Sweet potatoes
  • Popcorn kernels (light snack)

🥑 Healthy fats, nuts & extras

Heart, brain & joints

Omega-3 fats and nuts keep your heart and joints happy and help you feel full between meals.

  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Walnuts & almonds
  • Chia & ground flaxseed
  • Natural peanut/almond butter
  • Pumpkin seeds
  • Hummus
  • Cheese (string, feta)
  • Dark chocolate (70%+, treat)

🫖 Pantry, drinks & flavor

Hydration & anti-inflammatory seasonings

Staying hydrated is your #1 energy trick. Turmeric, ginger, and cinnamon add flavor and calm inflammation without salt.

  • Water — your main drink
  • Green & herbal tea
  • Low-sodium broth
  • Turmeric & black pepper
  • Fresh or ground ginger
  • Cinnamon
  • Canned tomatoes
  • Low-sodium seasonings/herbs

💃 Dance & activity fuel

Keep these on hand for the days you dance or are on your feet helping your friend:

Banana + peanut butter (before) Greek or soy yogurt + berries (after) Water with a squeeze of lemon Handful of almonds Orange for quick energy

A small protein + carb snack within an hour of dancing helps your muscles recover so you wake up less sore.

🍄 Your mushroom tinctures & supplements — keep your oncology team in the loop

Mom, your turkey tail and lion's mane dual-extracts are something to be proud of — turkey tail is even being studied in clinical trials for ER-positive breast cancer. The one important thing: medicinal mushrooms can change how hormone-blocker medicines are processed by the liver, and concentrated extracts put some load on the liver. So the smart move isn't to stop — it's to tell your oncologist and pharmacist the exact tinctures and daily doses you take, so they can keep an eye on it and your liver labs. Let your care team be your guide here, not the internet (or me 💛).

Whole foods vs. pills: whole soy foods (tofu, edamame, soy milk) are considered safe and even protective — but concentrated soy isoflavone supplements are a different thing; skip those unless your doctor okays them.

🦴 Bone & nutrient check — because of the hormone blockers

Hormone-blocker medicine can quietly thin bones over time, so bone-protective eating and a quick lab check matter for you. Foods above cover a lot of it, but at your next visit ask about Vitamin D, Calcium, and Vitamin B12 (harder to absorb with age, and lower on a plant-forward diet). Don't start supplements on your own — let your doctor tell you what you actually need.

🍽️ 5 fast, no-fuss meals

Power oatmeal
Oats + soy milk, stir in ground flax & chia, top with berries & walnuts. 5-min breakfast that lasts till lunch.
Desk-day lunch
Big salad: bagged greens, chickpeas, edamame, avocado, olive oil, seeds. No cooking. (Add chicken or salmon if you like.)
Tofu & greens stir-fry
Extra-firm tofu, frozen broccoli, garlic & ginger over brown rice. 15 min. (Salmon works here too.)
Lentil & veggie bowl
Canned lentils, roasted sweet potato, kale, tahini drizzle. Cozy and high-fiber.
Power plate
Hummus, avocado, edamame, whole-grain crackers, cherry tomatoes. Zero-cook. (Or canned salmon/tuna.)
Recovery bowl
Soy or Greek yogurt, banana, blueberries, pumpkin seeds & flax. Perfect post-dance.
