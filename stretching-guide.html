🧘 Your Gentle Stretching Guide

Brand-new to stretching? Start right here. Slow, simple, and easy on your back.

Mom, stretching should feel good — like a slow, pleasant lengthening, never a strain. This is a beginner's routine you can do in a chair or standing by a counter. Take it one stretch at a time, breathe the whole way through, and only go as far as feels comfortable. There's no "right" depth — your gentle is exactly right.
5 simple rules for happy stretching:
  • Warm up first. March in place or roll your shoulders for a minute so muscles are warm.
  • Move slowly into each stretch — no bouncing or jerking.
  • Breathe. Slow breath in through the nose, longer breath out. Never hold your breath.
  • Find a mild stretch, then pause. You want a gentle pull, never pain.
  • Little and often wins. A few minutes most days beats one long session.

💛 Because of your back pain — please read first

Keep everything in a slow, pain-free range. A gentle stretch is fine; a sharp, shooting, or burning feeling is your signal to ease off right away. Skip deep forward bends — the gentle, supported moves below are chosen to relieve a sore back, not stress it. Stop and call your doctor if you feel pain shooting down a leg, numbness, tingling, or weakness, or if back pain is new or getting worse. When in doubt, a physical therapist can tailor these to you.

About your nerve burns: because some areas may feel numb or "different," don't go by the feeling of a stretch to judge how far to move — you might not feel it stop you in time. Instead move in a small, slow, comfortable range and let your eyes and posture guide you, not the sensation. Please have your doctor or physical therapist okay this routine and show you how far is safe for your back and nerves.

🌸 A gentle note on the chest stretches

With breast implants — and any tightness from your past radiation — keep the chest and shoulder openers soft and small. A light, comfortable stretch across the chest is good for posture; if anything feels tender or tight, back off and let it open up gradually over time. Never force it.

🌼 Your 8-stretch starter routine

Tall sit & breathe

1Tall Sit & Breathe (warm-up)

  1. Sit toward the front of a sturdy chair, feet flat on the floor.
  2. Sit up tall, crown of the head lifting gently toward the ceiling, shoulders soft.
  3. Breathe in slowly through your nose for 4, out through your mouth for 6. Repeat 3–4 times.
3–4 breathsSets your posture and warms you up before you stretch.
Neck side stretch

2Gentle Neck Release

  1. Sitting tall, slowly tilt your right ear toward your right shoulder.
  2. Feel a soft stretch along the left side of your neck. Keep shoulders down.
  3. Hold and breathe, then float back to center and switch sides.
15 sec each sideMelts away neck and shoulder tension from desk work.
Shoulder rolls

3Shoulder Rolls

  1. Let your arms rest at your sides.
  2. Slowly circle both shoulders up toward your ears, back, and down — big, easy circles.
  3. Do 5 rolls backward, then 5 rolls forward.
5 each wayLoosens stiff shoulders and the upper back.
Seated cat–cow

4Seated Cat–Cow (back relief)

  1. Hands rest on your knees. Breathe in and gently arch — chest forward, look slightly up.
  2. Breathe out and gently round — tuck the chin, draw the belly back, curve the upper back.
  3. Flow slowly between the two with your breath. Keep it small and comfortable.
5 slow roundsA favorite for easing a stiff, sore back — gentle motion, no strain.
Gentle seated twist

5Gentle Seated Twist

  1. Sit tall, feet flat. Rest your right hand on your left knee.
  2. Breathe in to grow tall, breathe out and turn gently to the left — only as far as is comfy.
  3. Soften back to center, then twist to the other side. Lead with the breath, not the shoulders.
2 each sideKeeps your spine mobile — go easy and stay tall to protect your back.
Knee hug

6Seated Knee Hug (low-back relief)

  1. Sitting tall, gently draw one knee up toward your chest, hands behind the thigh.
  2. Give it a soft hug — you'll feel a gentle release in your lower back and hip.
  3. Lower slowly with control, then switch legs. Keep your back supported and upright.
15 sec each legA soothing release for a tight, achy lower back.
Gentle chest opener

7Gentle Chest Opener (keep it soft)

  1. Sit tall. Draw your shoulder blades gently together, opening across the chest.
  2. Let your hands turn palms-forward at your sides. Lift the chest just slightly.
  3. Breathe into the front of the chest. Keep it small and comfortable — ease off if tender.
10–15 secUndoes desk-hunch and lifts your posture — gentle is best for you here.
Ankle circles

8Ankle Circles & Point–Flex

  1. Lift one foot slightly off the floor.
  2. Slowly circle the ankle 5 times each direction.
  3. Then point the toes away, then flex them back. Switch feet.
5 circles each wayKeeps ankles supple and steady for walking and dancing.
Finish with a breath. Sit comfortably, hands resting in your lap, and take three slow breaths. Notice how your body feels a little looser and lighter. That's the whole point. 💛
